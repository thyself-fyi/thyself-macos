# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Thyself is a tool for self-understanding. The people who build it and the people who use it are engaging with deeply personal material. That demands an environment of genuine respect, care, and intellectual honesty — in the codebase, in issues, in discussions, and in how we treat each other.

## Standards

**Expected behavior:**

- Treat every contributor and user with respect and good faith
- Give constructive, specific feedback — critique the work, not the person
- Be honest about what you know and what you don't
- Respect the privacy of users — this software handles the most personal data imaginable
- Welcome newcomers and help them find their footing
- Disagree openly and respectfully when you see things differently

**Unacceptable behavior:**

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks of any kind
- Dismissive or demeaning language toward contributors at any experience level
- Publishing or threatening to publish others' private information
- Misuse of access to user data, test data, or contributor information
- Sustained disruption of discussions or development processes
- Any attempt to access, exfiltrate, or exploit user data processed by the Software

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, code reviews, and any communication channels associated with the project. It also applies when representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to **conduct@thyself.fyi**. All reports will be reviewed promptly and confidentially. The project maintainers reserve the right to remove, edit, or reject contributions and to temporarily or permanently ban any contributor for behavior they determine to be harmful.
